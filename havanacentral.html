Havana Central

  • June 15, 2018
  • By Josh and Josh

Saturday night in Havana Central is as crowded as you would expect for a restaurant in Times Square. Nevertheless, the Cuban food is good enough and not as overpriced as usual tourist traps in the area, making the place a hit for locals and tourists alike. It’s the music, though, that makes coming down to Havana Central worth it. One of the best musical venues to spend a night at in New York.

As locals, my friends and I usually don’t go anywhere near Times Square if we want to have a fun night out - the area is full of overly crowded, overpriced mediocre restaurants teeming with tourists who don’t want the authentic New York experience. I’m happy to say that after being persuaded to come to Havana Central by visiting friends, this place beats any other Times Square restaurant. First, the restaurant is two floors with generously tall ceilings and a good amount away from the main Times Square, so it wasn’t as claustrophobic as some other spaces nearby. However, it is still in the area, and we did have to wait a sizable amount of time (no reservations made), and when we were inside, we were shoved into a tiny, cheaply-made table wedged uncomfortably close to another party. I didn’t love the seating arrangement and all the waiters seemed so snappy and too busy to actually care about a small party such as ourselves, dampening our experience a little.

Despite that, the food and drinks were excellent and more reasonably priced than I expected. Once we could finally get a waiter to pay attention to us, the table shared amazing mojitos, and the paella and pollo asado were perfectly spiced and cooked. All the flavors seemed very authentically Cuban, a rarity in Midtown, and everyone was happy with the generous serving sizes.

The food alone was great, but what really made Havana Central more worthwhile than other Times Square restaurants was the live music. The atmosphere was made so lively by the colorful lights and the sheer happiness of the band - you could tell they loved the music they were playing and were having fun with it. There were drums, keyboard, a wonderful lead vocalist with incredible skill, clave, electric guitar, and more instruments I couldn’t recognize (we were pretty far away). The music was mostly salsa with upbeat melodies and loud rhythms - it was dancing music, but there was no room for anyone to dance, though I did see many happy patrons swaying and nodding to the beat, and a couple dancers near the front entrance. What made the experience great was the atmosphere the band provided - the vocalist and musicians would come down and talk to some folks, and they were always laughing and joking, giving the place a friendly, local vibe. We were quite far, so the music was perfectly loud, but I imagine those sitting right in front of the band felt a little deaf after a while. The food was quite good, but the band transformed the place into an authentic experience so enthralling you’ll forget you’re in Times Square.

In conclusion, the place is a nice change of scenery compared to the usual busy American restaurants around Times Square. Beware of long wait times and busy waiters, but altogether worth it if not just for the cheerful band and cheap mojitos.
