Barlovento

  • June 15, 2018
  • By Josh and Josh

A must-go if you want to listen to some good Cuban music and feel the electrifying energy of a crowded dance club without actually going to a dance club. Barlovento was truly an experience that was more than just a date night at any old restaurant. Downsides included mediocre American-Cuban meals that were more American than Cuban, but overall still an experience to partake in at least once.

A couple friends and I went to Barlovento on a whim without looking it up on Yelp as we were in the area for the night (Kips Bay area), and it was certainly not anything like we were expecting. Marketing itself as a “Latin Kitchen with a NYC Twist” with a mix of Latin and American food on the menu, we went in expecting just an hour or two of dinner and drinks. When we got in, we arrived in a dimly lit room and were seated in the very back away from what looked like a stage platform, though it was empty at the time. There was a decent crowd by the time we came, and the atmosphere was pleasant and lively.

To get the bad stuff out of the way first, the food was just okay, nothing special. We ordered a Cuban sandwich, chicken sandwich, and empanadas. The sandwiches were honestly dry and bland, and the meat in the Cuban seemed like luncheon cheap ham at best. The food wasn’t exactly authentic Latin, which was part of the charm of the “restaurant with an American twist,” but it ended up being neither here nor there and altogether forgettable. The best part of the meal was the margarita pitcher (quite cheap for New York restaurant standards), but other than that, I’d skip the meal next time and go for appetizers only or something other than the sandwiches.

I would still rate this restaurant highly, however, because of the entertainment. As we ate, a band came up to the stage and began playing, instantly livening up the crowd. The band was mostly playing some simple melodies, but they were energetic, upbeat, and pulsating to the rhythm. I wasn’t quite familiar with the music style, but instruments included keyboard, bass guitar, conga drums, claves, and even cowbells, with a main singer and a backup singer killing a couple songs, though it was mostly focused on the instruments. As we watched, a sizable crowd of excited folks formed at the front of the stage and began dancing to the music. It was a simple two-step dance, but everyone was chattering and the atmosphere was vibrant and full of spunk.

The music didn’t really vary much in style or skill, but that didn’t make it any less enjoyable throughout the night. The people in the audience really made the experience worthwhile; the beat was so catchy and the atmosphere was so bright that you couldn’t help but at least nod along. I can’t explain the vibe of the place, but if you go, you can never forget being there in the dim lighting, swaying to the music and drinking margarita. Everyone was having a great time - even the singer of the band came down to talk to some folks in the audience, which was really nice. All in all, a fun night I would do again… but order something different.
